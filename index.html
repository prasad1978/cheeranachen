Rev. Fr. Dr. Joseph Cheeran

Historian | Priest | Professor

Life Overview

Rev. Fr. Dr. Joseph Cheeran, also known as Cheeranachen, is a renowned church historian, orator, writer, and scholar of the Malankara Orthodox Church. Born in Pazhanji Village near Kunnamkulam, Trichur District, on August 17, 1945, he was raised by his father after losing his mother at the age of three. Despite these challenges, Fr. Cheeran earned First Rank in the Malayalam Vidwan Examination at the University of Kerala and later completed his Ph.D. from Mahatma Gandhi University. His thesis focused on 'The Influence of the Bible on Modern Malayalam Poetry. Fr.Cheeran is the research supervisor of Mahatma Gandhi University in Union Christian College, Alway and successfully guided Many Doctoral thesis for the Ph.D works'

Priest

Ordained as a priest by H.G. Yuhanon Mar Severiose, Fr. Cheeran served in several dioceses, including Kochi, Kunnamkulam, Trichur, and Angamaly. He was the vicar of numerous churches in the Malankara Orthodox Syrian Church (WestFort St.Ignatious Church,Trichur Chembukkavu St.Thomas Church, Nehru Nagar St.Gregoriose Church, Karippakkunnu- Mar Baseliose Church, Koratty St.Kuriakose Church, Chavarampadam, Manalithra St.John's church, Parambayi St.George Church, Akkikkavu St.Mary's Church, Perumthuruthy Mar Baseliose Church, Mangad St.Gregoriose Church, Kottol St.Gregoriose Church, Chowannoor St.Gregoriose Church, Kottapady, Kakkinikkad Catholicate Centre, Alayikalkulambu Church- Chelakkara) and currently a retired priest at St. Mary’s Orthodox Syrian Church in Pazhanji and St.Gregoriose Catholicate Center, Angamaly. He also founded the Yuhanon Mar Severiose Foundation and contributed as editor of ‘Sion Sandesam’ and ‘Edavaka Pathrika.’

Professor

Fr. Cheeran was a professor of Malayalam at Union Christian College, Alwaye, where he made significant contributions to academia. He has authored 56 books and published more than 400 research articles, primarily focusing on church history and literature. His works are well-respected for their depth of research and their uncovering of untold historical narratives.

Discoveries of Fr. Dr. Joseph Cheeran in Church History

Fr. Cheeran's research unearthed crucial manuscripts, shedding light on the early history of the Malankara Orthodox Church. His most notable discoveries include the *Sustatikon* of Mar Gregoriose Abdul Jaleel (1665), Mar Ivaniose (1685), and the liturgical book of Mafriyana Yelodo Mar Baseliose (1685). He also uncovered documents like the St. Thomas Bible used in India and the *Hudaya Canon* manuscript, which had previously been buried in libraries.

Encyclopedic Works

Fr. Cheeran contributed to numerous encyclopedia works, most notably the Indian Orthodox Saba Charithra Vinjana Kosham , a comprehensive encyclopedia detailing the history and liturgical traditions of the Indian Orthodox Church. This monumental work remains a key reference for scholars of church history.

Syriac Language Studies

As a scholar fluent in Syriac (Western and Eastern), Fr. Cheeran explored and translated ancient Syriac manuscripts, contributing significantly to understanding early Christian liturgical practices. His translations and analyses have made Syriac texts more accessible to modern scholars.

Biblical Studies

Fr. Cheeran's Ph.D. thesis, titled The Influence of the Bible on Modern Malayalam Poetry, reflects his profound interest in the Bible's impact on literature. His works on Biblical studies, including the Bharathathilae Pracheena Bible, provide a deep analysis of early Bible translations in India.
